3. Soal essay bahasa inggris tentang Accepting and refusing beserta jawaban ​


Dalam percakapan, kita terkadang sering dihadapkan oleh situasi menerima atau menolak saran ataupun ajakan. Demikian pula dalam bahasa Inggris, hanya saja pemilihan dan penyusunan kata dalam bahasa Inggris untuk menerima (accepting) dan menolak (refusing) agak berbeda dengan bahasa Indonesia. Mari kita simak contoh dari refusing dan  accepting.

Pembahasan  

1. Contoh jika situasi nya adalah ajakan atau undangan secara formal :

"I would like to invite you to the annual party on this Saturday "

(Saya bermaksud untuk menggundang anda untuk hadir diacara tahunan hari sabtu ini)  

Accepting:

Thank you for inviting me, I am honored.

(Terimakasih sudah mengundang saya, saya tersanjung)  

Refusing :

Thank you for the invitation, but I am truly sorry, I have already had another appointment this Saturday.

(Terimakasih atas undangannya, tapi saya benar-benar minta maaft, Saya sudah ada janji sabtu ini.  

Dalam menolak ajakan, biasanya kita memulai dengan kata terimakasih kemudian disertai alasan mengapa kita menolak ajakan tersebut.

2. Contoh jika situasi nya adalah ajakan atau undangan secara informal :

"Hi, I will have a barbecue party this weekend, do you want to come?"

(Hai, aku akan mengadakan pesta barbecue weekend ini, mau datang?)  

Accepting :

That's sounds great, I'll be there. Thanks anyway.

(menyenangkan sekali, aku akan kesans)  

Refusing :

I'd love to, but I have to go to see my grandma this weekend, She's not feeling well.

(Aku sebenarnya ingin, tapi aku harus mengunjungi nenekku weekend ini, beliau sedikit kurang enak badan)  

3. Contoh jika situasi nya adalah saran (suggestion) secara formal :

"What if we consider a new way of marketing? "

(bagaimana kalau kita mempertimbangkan cara baru dalam pemasaran)  

Accepting:

"Agreed. I think we need some innovation"  

(Setuju. Saya rasa kita memerlukan inovasi)

Refusing :

"That's sounds good, but what is wrong with the old one? I think it is already perfect."  

(Sarannya bagus juga, tapi ada apa dengan cara yang lama? Aku rasa cara yang lama itu sudah sangat sempurna)

4. Contoh jika situasi nya adalah saran (suggestion) secara informal :

"I think you look good with this color"  

(aku rasa kamu cocok pakai warna ini

"really? That's what I thought too"  

(benarkah? Aku juga berpikir seperti itu)  

"I think it's too bright for me, I am not confidence to wear it"

(aku rasa itu terlalu terang untuk ku, aku tidak percaya diri memakainya)

Dalam menolak saran, biasanya kita awali dengan memuji saran tersebut, contoh, "That's sound preefty cool but...", "Not bad, but i think..."

*Hal yang harus diperhatikan dalam membuat, menolak, menerima saran atau ajakan adalah bentuk yang di pakai selalu dalam bentuk present. Dan biasanya terdapat awalan kalimat yang menunjukkan kesopanan seperti " I would like...", " would love to but...", "Thank you for...", "I would appreciate that...",  "I am glad to...", "I am honoured", "I would be happy if..."

perbedaan job dan profesi dalam bahasa Inggris​

Job:

as a "noun" means a task or piece of work, especially one that is paid.as a "verb" means do casual or occasional work.

Profession (Noun):

A profession is an occupation founded upon specialized educational training.

Penjelasan:

Jawaban dicetak tebal dan merupakan materi pembelajaran Vocabulary (kosa kata) dalam Bahasa Inggris.

Kata "job" yang memiliki arti pekerjaan dalam Bahasa Inggris terbagi menjadi dua:

Kata benda (Noun) yang berarti sebuah tugas atau pekerjaan, terutama yang dibayar.Kata kerja (Verb) yang berarti melakukan pekerjaan santai / kasual atau sesekali waktu.

Kata "profession" yang memiliki arti profesi adalah sebuah kata benda yang merupakan suatu pekerjaan yang dilakukan berdasarkan pelatihan atau pendidikan khusus.

10. how write a job application letter?


1. Write the contact information at the top.
2. Address the letter with a name or title.
3. Express your interest in the first paragraph. State where you found the position, and how you'll benefit the employer.
4. Summarize your strengths, qualifications, and experience in the second paragraph.
5. Conclude with a one-sentence summary of why you’re the perfect candidate.
6. Invite the hiring manager to contact you.
7. Sign off appropriately, and write your name.

Watch the time!

First, pay attention to what time you contact that person. It's not too early or too late, so it's best to call the person between 8:00 and 19:00.

In addition, it is also important to know the schedule of the people you will contact, for example there are people who do not want to be contacted on Sundays because of famtime, or your messages will not be answered at lunchtime.

Call or Text?

Well, actually it's okay to contact someone directly by phone. But, just in case you should make sure via short message first, the goal is to make sure that the person is not busy and can respond to you at the right time.

Text Properly!

Now this is the most crucial and often wrong way to do it by young people today. Called ten at night, use informal language. Fix your message will not be replied to! To avoid that, you need to know the proper way to contact someone you don't know.

1. Say hello or say hello politely.

good manners before expressing the purpose of contacting, you can say hello or say hello, because it will seem to appreciate the person.

For example: Good morning sir, or Assalamualaikum sir

2. Get used to using apologize, excuse me and please.

We don't know the exact schedule of the person we will contact, that's why before starting the introduction and the purpose of your contact, you need to say "sorry to disturb" or "please"

3. Introduce yourself or show your identity clearly.

You don't want your message to be ignored because he doesn't know who is calling. Well, it's not just you who contacted him, so it's really important to say your name and where you come from

For example: I am Rizka from grade 12 IPA 3, or I am Renanta Rizka from Brilio.

4. Note the correct punctuation and capital letters.

You don't have to use the complete PUEBI, match the person you will contact. But imagine you are reading a sentence without commas or periods, people who read it can get confused and even cause misunderstandings. Or you love your credit too much so you cut up the contents of the message. BIG NO!

For example: I want to ask if you will teach an Introduction to Communication class tomorrow? If you don't teach, do you still have any assignments, sir?

5. Explain your purpose for contacting.

explain your needs in a concise, clear, polite and to the point. When making an appointment, it's best not to use commanding sentences, but instead use offer sentences, or ask directly for the time and place.

For example: I am working on an article about an exam, so I need to interview Mr.Rendy to be my resource. Are you willing?

end with thanks

Saying thanks is the same as appreciating the conversation with the person and paying respect, so it's very important to say thank you at the end of a sentence.

Patient is Must!

After obeying the procedures for sending messages to someone you don't know, the next thing is don't terrorize, bomb or send messages over and over again! Apart from being uncomfortable, sending chats repeatedly is also impolite because it seems pushy.

Penjelasan:

Application Letter

Application letter adalah surat bisnis yang bertujuan untuk melamar pekerjaan kedalam sebuah perusahaan.

Generic Structure dari Application Letter adalah:

1. salutation : Berisi salam pembuka kepada HRD perusahaan atau orang yang bertanggung jawab dalam perekrutan.

2. introduction : Berisi pembukaan. Biasanya berbentuk perkenalan diri atau biodata pelamar.

3. main message or body: Berisi maksud dan tujuan penulis bahwa dia akan melamar sebuah pekerjaan dalam perusahaan tersebut.

4. the close : Berisi kalimat penutupan. Biasanya berbentuk harapan agar si penulis dapat diterima dalam perusahaan tersebut.

5. complimentary close: Berisi salam penutup dan ucapan terimakasih.

Language features dari Application Letter:

1. use the present tense

2. use the verbs to indicate actions and feelings

3. use the future tense for suggesting solutions to problems the job
