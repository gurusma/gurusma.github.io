2. Buatlah contoh text cerita rakyat, legenda dan mitos dalam bahasa inggris beserta social function, generic structure and language feature​


Title: The Legend of Malin Kundang

Social Function: To entertain and educate readers about a famous Indonesian legend, imparting moral lessons about filial piety and consequences of one's actions.

Generic Structure:

Orientation: Introduce the setting, characters, and initial situation.

Complication: Present the conflict or dilemma faced by the main character.

Resolution: Describe the climax and its consequences.

Reorientation: Conclude the story and deliver the moral lesson.


Language Features:

Use of descriptive language to create imagery and evoke emotions.

Dialogue between characters to convey their thoughts and emotions.

Cultural and local references to establish the setting.

Repetition of moral lessons for emphasis.

Use of traditional storytelling devices like repetition and imagery.

The Legend of Malin Kundang

Once upon a time, in a small coastal village in West Sumatra, there lived a young man named Malin Kundang. He was known for his handsome looks and his dream to escape his humble origins and seek wealth and glory in far-off lands. His mother, a hardworking and loving woman, always warned him to stay humble and never forget his roots.

One day, Malin Kundang set sail on a grand voyage, promising his mother that he would return home as a successful man. Years passed, and he amassed wealth beyond imagination. He married a beautiful princess and became a rich and powerful king.

As he sailed back to his village with his lavish entourage, he saw his mother waiting by the shore. Ashamed of his humble origins, he ordered his crew to sail away, ignoring his mother's desperate cries for recognition.

Suddenly, a massive storm swept over the sea. Lightning struck his ship, and it turned to stone. Malin Kundang and his crew were transformed into rocks for his disobedience and disrespect toward his mother.

Moral of the Story:

This legend teaches us the importance of respecting our parents and not letting pride and arrogance overshadow our roots. It also conveys the idea that actions have consequences, and that one should never forget where they come from.

9. sebutkan social function,language features,stucture text dari materi sign​


Function :

• Report text is a text which present information about something, as it is. It is as a result of systematic observation and analyses.

• Its social purpose is presenting information about something. They generally describe an entire class of things, whether natural or made: mammals, the planets, rocks, plants, countries of region, culture, transportation, and so on.

Generic Structure of Report Text :

• General Classification: General statements that describe the subject of the report, description, and classification.

• Description: tells what the phenomenon under discussion ; in terms of parts, qualities, habits or behaviors.

Language features of Report text :

• Nouns and noun phrases are used rather than personal pronouns. The use of personal pronouns is limited.

• Most reports are written in the present tense.

• Use some passive voice

• Some reports use technical or scientific terms.

• Linking verbs are used, eg. is, are, has, have, belong to, to give coherence.

• Uses some action verbs (climb, eat).

• Descriptive language is used that is factual rather than imaginative eg. color, shape, size, body parts, habits, behaviors, functions, uses.

Example of Report Text :

A bicycle, is called a bike or cycle, is a human-powered, pedal-driven, single-track vehicle, having two wheels attached to a frame, one behind the other. A bicycle rider is called a cyclist, or bicyclist.

Bicycles were introduced in the 19th century in Europe and as of 2003, more than 1 billion have been produced worldwide, twice as many as the number of automobiles that have been produced. They are the principal means of transportation in many regions. They also provide a popular form of recreation, and have been adapted for use as children's toys, general fitness, military and police applications, courier services, and bicycle racing.

28. Bagaimana menemukan perbedaan "figurative language" antara bahasa Indonesia dan bahasa Inggris?. kalau ada yang tau mohon bantuannya.


Figurative language di bahasa Indonesia dan bahasa Inggris konsepnya sama, ada konotasi atau makna kiasan dan denotasi atau makna lugas. Yang berbeda hanya idiom-idiomnya saja, contoh dalam bahasa Indonesia orang bodoh disebut dengan otak udang, sementara dalam bahasa Inggris disebut dengan airheaded (kepala angin, isinya angin saja tidak ada otaknya). Idiom-idiom ini tidak ada rumus baku tertentu dan memang harus dihafalkan, namun konsep dasar figurative language kedua bahasa ini sama.

The Presentation

This page is about the actual presentation itself as opposed to other aspects of giving presentations in English.

Most presentations are divided into 3 main parts (+ questions):

1 INTRODUCTION (Questions)

2 BODY

3 CONCLUSION

Questions

As a general rule in communication, repetition is valuable. In presentations, there is a golden rule about repetition:

Say what you are going to say...

say it...

then say what you have just said.

In other words, use the three parts of your presentation to reinforce your message. In the introduction, you tell your audience what your message is going to be. In the body, you tell your audience your real message. In the conclusion, you summarize what your message was.

We will now consider each of these parts in more detail.

Introduction

The introduction is a very important - perhaps the most important - part of your presentation. This is the first impression that your audience have of you. You should concentrate on getting your introduction right. You should use the introduction to:

welcome your audience

introduce your subject

outline the structure of your presentation

give instructions about questions
